Bio

Dr. Kaiyang Zhou is currently a postdoctoral researcher at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore, working with Prof. Ziwei Liu and Prof. Chen Change Loy. He obtained his PhD in Computer Science (2020) from the University of Surrey, UK, under the supervision of Prof. Tao Xiang. Prior to that, he received his MSc with Distinction (2016) from the University of Bristol, UK; BSc with 1st class honor (2015) from Ulster University, UK; BEng (2015) from Fujian Normal University, China. He has worked in Samsung AI Center Cambridge, UK, as a research intern from 2018–19, and Shenzhen Institute of Advanced Technology (SIAT), Chinese Academy of Sciences, as a visiting student supervised by Prof. Yu Qiao from 2016–17.

Dr. Zhou's research centers around the development of generalizable machine learning algorithms for computer vision, with particular interests in the following topics: domain generalization/adaptation, transfer learning, semi-supervised learning, X-shot learning (X=zero/few), and unsupervised learning. His papers have been published at major journals and conferences in the field, including TPAMI (IF: 17.861), TIP (IF: 10.865), ICLR (#1 conference in AI), AAAI, and the three prominent computer vision conferences (CVPR, ICCV & ECCV). According to Google Scholar, his papers have been cited more than 1,300 times, with h-index at 13 (as of June 2022). He serves as a reviewer for top-tier venues in the field including TPAMI, ICLR, NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR, ICCV, ECCV, and so on.

Dr. Zhou is also an active contributor to the open-source community on Github and has developed several open-source projects that are widely used by the community, of both academia and industry, with over 5.9k stars received in total. Two representative projects are Torchreid, for person re-identification, and Dassl.pytorch, for domain generalization.
